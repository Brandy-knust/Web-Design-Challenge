Summary: Cloudiness vs Latitude

Relating the Cloudiness to the Distance from the Equator

This plot analyzes cloudiness and latitude. It seems to imply that most of the cloudiness is measure by percent cloud cover which is why there are lines at each of the multiples of 20 or 25. It is easy to measure by saying 20 or 25 percent cloud cover.

There is, however no apparent relationship between cloud cover and latitude, which makes sense. Cloud cover is an indicator of day to day weather. Other than coastal cities that experience rain nearly every day of the year, most areas will have varying cloud cover based on daily conditions, not based on latitude. Many times it is either very clear, which is why there are so many points at zero, or there is a varying amount of cloud cover, which is difficult to describe since it changes throughout the day.
